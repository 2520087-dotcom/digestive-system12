The Human Digestive System

The human digestive system is a group of organs that work together to break down food and absorb nutrients. It includes the mouth, stomach, intestines, and several other important organs.

Introduction

Digestion begins in the mouth and continues through the stomach and intestines. Each organ plays a special role in converting food into energy for the body.

Organs Information

The table below provides basic information about some major digestive organs including their function and approximate position in the body.

Organ Function Position
Mouth Breaks food into smaller pieces and mixes it with saliva. Head
Stomach Breaks down food using acid and enzymes. Upper Abdomen
Small Intestine Absorbs most of the nutrients from food. Middle Abdomen
Large Intestine Absorbs water and forms waste. Lower Abdomen
